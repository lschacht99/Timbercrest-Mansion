The concept

Everyone you love.
Under one roof.

Timbercrest isn't a hotel, and it isn't a typical rental. It's four historic mansions built for one thing: gathering people — 18, 32, or a hundred of them — minutes from Mount Snow.

Most trips split a group across hotel rooms, hallways and group chats. Here, the whole point is the opposite — one kitchen, one long table, one fire, and enough bedrooms that nobody sleeps on a couch.

The houses Jack built

1980s

1980s

Jack builds big

Local legend Jack Ridgeway, a pioneer of the vacation-rental industry, builds these estates at the foot of Mount Snow with his wife Margit — at a scale nobody else dared: huge rooms, abundant common areas, craftsmanship in every detail.

Decades of gatherings

Decades of gatherings

Traditions take root

Families return year after year. One family has celebrated Thanksgiving here for 26 years and counting. Reunions, ski weeks, weddings — the houses become the backdrop of people's lives.

Today

Today

Restored, not replaced

Each mansion is thoughtfully updated — modern amenities, restored carriage barn, on-site managers Michelle & Ryan — while keeping the timeless character Jack built into the walls.
